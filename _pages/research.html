I study state and local public finance, economic development, and how local institutions absorb economic shocks. My work uses applied econometrics on administrative data to evaluate the role of local financial institutions, entrepreneurship, and public-sector capacity in moderating the effects of crises — extreme weather, financial shocks, and the opioid epidemic, primarily — on regional economies. Substantively, I sit at the intersection of public administration, regional economics, and public health.

Currently working on

  • Minority-owned banks and community resilience to financial crises (with M. Feldman et al.)
  • Extreme weather and labor market outcomes by race and ethnicity
  • Hays County public finance and growth along the I-35 corridor
  • Cost-benefit analysis for the Town of Clayton, NC (ArcLight Analytics)
