W. Scott Langford

Assistant Professor · Texas State University

I study state and local public finance and economic development — how institutions absorb shocks, finance growth, and govern the corridors that connect them. I write Southbound 35, a blog on the Texas I-35 corridor.

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Political Science at Texas State University, in the MPA program. I was previously a post-doctoral research fellow in the School of Public Affairs at Arizona State, and earned my PhD in Public Policy from UNC Chapel Hill. My research has appeared in the Journal of Business Venturing Insights, Economic Development Quarterly, the Journal of Regional Science, and JAMA Pediatrics.

I am open to consulting, invited talks, and media inquiries. The best way to reach me is email.
